$$L = \sum_i \frac{1}{2}m_i\dot{r}_i^2 + \sum_i \frac{1}{2}I_i\dot{\theta}_i^2 + \sum_{i<j}\frac{Gm_im_j}{r_{ij}}\left[1 + \varepsilon_m \Xi_{ij}\cos\left(\frac{\Delta\theta_{ij}}{2}\right)\right] - \sum_{i<j}\frac{\kappa}{r_{ij}^\nu}\left(1 - \cos\left(\frac{\Delta\theta_{ij}}{2}\right)\right)$$

## 4. Limiting Cases: The Unification Table

### 4.1 Reduction → Newton (Classical Mechanics)

**Conditions:** $\varepsilon_m \to 0$ (no phase feedback on gravity), and/or $\Delta\theta_{ij} \to 0$ ⟹ cos(Δθ/2) → 1 and κ → 0 (no phase dynamics)

**Result:**
$$m_i\ddot{r}_i = \sum_{j\neq i}\frac{Gm_im_j}{r_{ij}^3}(r_j - r_i), \qquad I_i\ddot{\theta}_i = 0$$

You recover **exactly Newton** (phases become inert constants).

### 4.2 Reduction → General Relativity (Weak Field Limit)

**Conditions:** $|\Delta\theta_{ij}| \ll 1$, so:
$$\cos\left(\frac{\Delta\theta_{ij}}{2}\right) \approx 1 - \frac{(\Delta\theta_{ij})^2}{8}$$

If phases remain quasi-aligned (parallel adiabatic transport) and κ/r^ν large enough to lock Δθ ≈ 0:

$$U_{ij} \simeq -\frac{Gm_im_j}{r_{ij}}\left[1 + \varepsilon_m \Xi_{ij}\right] = -\frac{G_{eff,ij}m_im_j}{r_{ij}}$$

with $G_{eff,ij} = G(1 + \varepsilon_m \Xi_{ij})$ constant if Q, R constant at the considered scale.

In the Newtonian regime of GR (weak metric g₀₀ ≃ -1 + 2Φ/c²), dynamics depend only on potential Φ. Here, Φ is **renormalized** in G → G_eff, without violating conservation.

**Result:** The theory reduces to GR's weak-field Newtonian limit with an effective G—an integrable limit case without explicit curvature.

### 4.3 Reduction → MOND-like (Very Low Acceleration Regime)

**Mechanism:** MOND modifies the law a·μ(a/a₀) = a_N. Here, we can **mimic** MOND-like behavior if:
- ε_m small but non-zero
- κ/r^ν induces distance-dependent phase locks (for ν > 0) causing a non-linear response of cos(Δθ/2) with r

**Key idea:** In the stationary phase state:
$$\sum_{j\neq i}\frac{\kappa}{r_{ij}^\nu}\sin\left(\frac{\Delta\theta_{ij}}{2}\right) = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \Delta\theta_{ij} = \Delta\theta_{ij}(r_{ij})$$

Then the effective force becomes:
$$F_{ij} \propto \frac{1}{r_{ij}^2}\left[1 + \varepsilon_m \Xi_{ij}\cos\left(\frac{\Delta\theta_{ij}(r_{ij})}{2}\right)\right]$$

This defines a **modified radial law**, potentially flat (or less steep) at large distance depending on ν and the stationary profile of Δθ(r).

**Result:** The structure can reproduce a MOND-like regime (without axiomatizing it) through **emergence** of phase-distance coupling. This is a coherent phenomenological limit, not ad hoc parameterization.

### 4.4 Reduction → Schrödinger / Quantum Hydrodynamics (Madelung)

**Mechanism:** Identify θ_i as **reduced local action** (analog S/ℏ), and pass to the continuum (density ρ, velocity field v = ∇S/m).

For a pair (or distribution), writing the average phase equation:
$$\partial_t\theta + \frac{(\nabla S)^2}{2m} + V_{eff}(\rho, \theta) = 0$$

If a term ∝ ∇²√ρ/√ρ appears in V_eff via closure (choice ν = 2 and local coupling), you recover the **Madelung structure** (hydrodynamic equations equivalent to Schrödinger):

$$\begin{cases} \partial_t\rho + \nabla\cdot(\rho v) = 0 \\ m(\partial_t v + v\cdot\nabla v) = -\nabla(V + Q), \quad Q = -\frac{\hbar_{eff}^2}{2m}\frac{\nabla^2\sqrt{\rho}}{\sqrt{\rho}} \end{cases}$$

**Result:** Under adequate closure (continuization + choice ν + scale I, κ), phase dynamics gives an **effective quantum term Q**: quantum hydrodynamic reduction. The theory **contains** a Schrödinger/Madelung limit when phase becomes a collective field.

### 4.5 Reduction → Kuramoto / HMF (Phase Oscillators)

**Mechanism:** Freeze positions r_ij ≈ constant (or quasi-uniform distribution) and keep only θ dynamics:

$$I_i\ddot{\theta}_i = -\sum_{j\neq i}K_{ij}\sin\left(\frac{\Delta\theta_{ij}}{2}\right), \qquad K_{ij} := \frac{\kappa}{2r_{ij}^\nu}$$

This is a **generalized Kuramoto Hamiltonian** (close to HMF), with coupling sin(Δθ/2) instead of sin(Δθ).

**Result:** The theory **encompasses** synchronization models as a pure phase limit (frozen space).
